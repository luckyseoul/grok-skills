# Scientific Critique

Produce a cold, multi-perspective evaluation of technical claims, methods, and evidence.

## Rubric Dimensions (score 1-5 + evidence)

1. Novelty / prior-art positioning
2. Methodological rigor and validity ranges
3. Evidence quality and traceability (primary data vs secondary)
4. Quantitative consistency (units, dimensionless groups, uncertainty)
5. Clarity and reproducibility of procedure
6. Significance / leverage of claimed advance
7. Limitations and threat-to-validity analysis
8. Internal consistency across abstract/methods/results/claims
9. Ethical / disclosure completeness (funding, data availability, conflicts)

## Workflow

1. Extract core claims and supporting evidence locations.
2. Map claims to sources; apply litreview counting discipline (only session-retrieved or explicitly labeled).
3. Score each dimension with concrete anchors (quotes, equation numbers, figure references, correlation limits).
4. Identify causal gaps or unstated assumptions that break the chain from mechanism to performance number.
5. Generate gap analysis and recommended next measurements or literature searches.
6. Optional multi-agent perspectives: editor, domain expert, devil’s advocate, patent examiner.

## Output

- Scored table with evidence snippets.
- Overall readiness assessment (publication, provisional filing, further work required).
- Prioritized list of high-leverage fixes or missing anchors.
- Evidence log suitable for patent file history or manuscript revision.

Flag all model extrapolations and unverifiable leaps. Prefer quantitative over qualitative judgments.
